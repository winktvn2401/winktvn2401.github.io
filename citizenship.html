    Citizenship by conferral

    Becoming an Australian Citizen By Conferral

    Citizenship by conferral is a pathway available to individuals holding permanent residency in Australia. This process involves applying for Australian citizenship and meeting the eligibility criteria established by the Australian government.

    Seeking professional advice from immigration experts is recommended to ensure a smooth and successful application process for obtaining Australian citizenship through conferral.

    Australian Citizenship  Eligibility Requirements

    To apply for Australian citizenship by conferral, applicants must meet several key requirements, including:

    • Holding Australian permanent residency

    • Having lived in Australia for at least four years, with absences of less than 12 months in total

    • Residing in Australia as a permanent visa holder for the past 12 months, with absences of less than 90 days

    • Demonstrating good character

    • Having adequate knowledge of the responsibilities and privileges of Australian citizenship

    • Intending to reside in Australia or maintain a close connection with the country.

    The Australian Citizenship Test

    The Australian Citizenship Test is an essential part of the citizenship application process. It consists of multiple-choice questions designed to assess an applicant's knowledge of Australian government, law, national symbols, and Indigenous culture. To meet the citizenship requirements, applicants must achieve a passing score on the test.

    Australian Citizenship Ceremony

    The Australian Citizenship Ceremony marks the final step in the journey to becoming an Australian citizen and is a significant celebration for new citizens. During the ceremony, participants take three pledges:

    I affirm my loyalty to Australia and its people,
    Whose democratic beliefs I share,
    Whose rights and liberties I respect,
    And whose laws I uphold and obey.

    Invitations are sent approximately four weeks before the ceremony, with local councils organizing the date and venue.
